# Cash funds and registry money goals

**Article ID:** HC-013  
**Audience:** Registry Owners and Registry Participants

Some registries include **cash fund** sections, pots with a target or instructions (for example “help with moving costs”). They help **Registry Participants** understand **what kind of monetary help** is welcome.

---

## Beabr does not move money for you (baseline product)

In the current product direction:

- Beabr is **not** a bank, e wallet, or payment processor.  
- Logging a “pledge” or similar entry is **not** the same as moving cleared funds through Beabr.  
- **Registry Participants** still arrange payment **however you privately agree** (bank transfer, e wallet, cash in person, and similar), subject to local law and personal safety.  

Some instances may **disable** certain pledge routes while keeping **display** of funds for planning. **Follow what your app actually allows** (error messages, disabled buttons). If the product blocks a path, it is intentional.

---

## Optional aggregate visibility before reveal

**Registry Owners** may have a setting about whether **aggregate** pledge totals for funds show before reveal **without** exposing individual payers. That setting does not override other privacy rules (for example identity hiding). See **[Pledge and total visibility](HC-031-pledge-visibility.md)**.

---

## Compared to **group gift pledges** on an item

**Group pledges** attach to a **specific gift line item** and may involve:

- A **Pledge Initiator** posting payout cues (account references, instructions, optional QR)  
- **Registry Participants** recording amounts and receipt photos  

That path is **especially sensitive** because it can involve **bank-like details and receipt imagery**. Read **[Group pledges and sensitive payout information](HC-024-pledges-group-gifts-sensitive-info.md)** end to end.

Cash funds are often **simpler conceptually** (instructions plus optional manual logs) but still require **care outside** payment apps.

---

## Tax, charity, and legal labels

If you represent that money is for charity, tax-deductible purposes, regulated crowdfunding, or employment-linked collections, **you** are responsible for accuracy and compliance. Beabr only provides software.
