# Set your reveal date and time

**Article ID:** HC-012  
**Audience:** Registry Owners (**Registry Participants:** read the section at the bottom)

The **reveal moment** is when the countdown you set reaches **zero**, or equivalently when "now" is **on or after** your configured reveal **date and time** for the registry (checked consistently for everyone using the live product clocks).

Registries have a setup-time gift visibility choice:

- **Private surprise** hides who reserved gifts, who marked gifts prepared, and who participated in pooled item pledges from **Registry Owners** until reveal. These protections are enforced by the running product, not only hidden in the layout.
- **Open coordination** shows attribution earlier so invited loved ones can avoid duplicates and coordinate gifts more directly.

This visibility choice is made when the registry is created and cannot be changed later.

---

## Choosing a sensible time

- Pick a timezone-safe moment. You see local times in UI, but correctness depends on clarity of scheduling in your jurisdiction's norms.
- For private surprise registries, communicate etiquette: **Registry Participants** should assume quiet preparation until reveal.
- Changing reveal time may have emotional or fairness implications. Only adjust with care.

---

## What changes after reveal

After reveal, contributor attribution becomes visible to the **Registry Owner** for private surprise registries. Open coordination registries may already show that attribution. Handle identities and any money-related details responsibly (no harassment, no coercion, no misuse of private information).

See **[After reveal](HC-014-after-reveal.md)** and **[Privacy summary](HC-030-privacy-reveal-summary.md)**.
For what Beabr is responsible for when misuse occurs, read **[Beabr's responsibility](../legal/beabr-responsibility.md)**.

---

## For Registry Participants reading this article

If a registry uses private surprise, respect it: avoid pressuring **Registry Owners** to circumvent rules, gossip-deanonymizing early based on deliveries, or screen-scraping what the product intentionally withholds. Open coordination is for groups that intentionally prefer visibility so loved ones can coordinate.
