# Set your reveal date and time

**Article ID:** HC-012  
**Audience:** Registry Owners (**Registry Participants:** read the section at the bottom)

The **reveal moment** is when the countdown you set reaches **zero**, or equivalently when “now” is **on or after** your configured reveal **date and time** for the registry (checked consistently for everyone using the live product clocks).

Until reveal, the product hides who reserved gifts, who marked gifts prepared, and who participated in pooled item pledges from **Registry Owners**. These protections are enforced by the running product, not only hidden in the layout.

Those protections exist so preparation stays discreet when you chose that rhythm.

---

## Choosing a sensible time

- Pick a timezone-safe moment. You see local times in UI, but correctness depends on clarity of scheduling in your jurisdiction’s norms.  
- Communicate etiquette: **Registry Participants** should assume **silent prep** until reveal.  
- Changing reveal time may have emotional or fairness implications. Only adjust with care.  

---

## What changes after reveal

After reveal, contributor attribution becomes visible to the **Registry Owner** for protected categories so thank you messages and closure are possible. Handle identities and any money-related details responsibly (no harassment, no coercion, no misuse of private information).

See **[After reveal](HC-014-after-reveal.md)** and **[Privacy summary](HC-030-privacy-reveal-summary.md)**.
For what Beabr is responsible for when misuse occurs, read **[Beabr’s responsibility](../legal/beabr-responsibility.md)**.

---

## For Registry Participants reading this article

Reveal exists to protect surprises. Respect it: avoid pressuring **Registry Owners** to circumvent rules, gossip-deanonymizing early based on deliveries, or screen-scraping what the product intentionally withholds. Attempts might violate **[Terms of use](../legal/terms-of-service.md)** and harm trust.
