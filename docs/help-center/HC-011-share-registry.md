# Share your registry

**Article ID:** HC-011  
**Audience:** Registry Owners

Your registry is **private**: it is not browsable like a public storefront. People join when **you** invite them.

---

## Join code

You receive a **short code** **Registry Participants** can type into the Join flow after signing in.

**Treat the code like a secret link.** Anyone who gets the code may be able to become a **Registry Participant** unless future product controls add revocation or invitation limits. Follow **inside the app** tooling if available.

If you suspect the code leaked, regenerate or pause sharing according to **help or settings** in your instance, or create a successor registry strategy if rotate is unsupported.

---

## Shareable link

You also get a **URL** **Registry Participants** can tap. Links are convenient but have the **same secrecy** implication as codes.

### Tips

- Send through channels you trust (private group chats, email to known recipients).  
- Avoid posting publicly if you intend the registry to stay among a defined circle.  
- Remind recipients **not** to re-share wildly if you rely on secrecy before reveal day.  

---

## What invitees experience

Recipients sign in the same way you did (often Google or email one time codes), open your link or enter the code, and become members of **that registry only**. Invitees ordinarily cannot rewrite registry-wide settings unless the product defines a broader role later.
