# After reveal: seeing contributors

**Article ID:** HC-014  
**Audience:** Registry Owners

After the reveal date and time, Beabr shows contributor attribution that was protected by private surprise mode. If the registry uses open coordination, some attribution may already be visible before this moment.

---

## What you see after reveal

After reveal, the reveal view can include:

- Names tied to gifts marked prepared
- Notes attached to prepared gifts
- Pooled pledge details for items, including who contributed and receipt status where that feature is enabled
- Cash fund pledges and their amounts, when cash fund pledges are enabled

The authoritative behavior is enforced inside the running product, not by hiding rows only in the browser layout.

---

## Responsibilities now that you see personal data

- **Minimal use:** thank contributors and coordinate kindly. Do not repurpose contact details or stories for unrelated outreach.
- **Security:** screenshots containing payout references or receipts are sensitive. Protect your devices.
- **Disputes:** money disagreements belong between **Registry Participants** and banks; Beabr logs are coordination aids.

Read **[Terms of use](../legal/terms-of-service.md)** for **Registry Owner** responsibilities toward **Registry Participant** data after reveal.

---

## Thank you notes

You can compose thank-you letters inside Beabr. Keep messages respectful, specific, and grateful. See **[Send a thank you note](HC-015-thank-you.md)**.

---

## If something looks wrong

If totals look inconsistent with banking reality:

1. **Registry Participants** reconcile using their bank statements first.
2. Use diplomacy. Often errors are misunderstandings about currency fees or partial transfers.
3. Escalate suspected fraud through proper banking fraud channels, not through harassment inside thank-you tooling.

Report security vulnerabilities, including unintended identity leaks, to the developer at **keith_tagarao01@yahoo.com.ph**.
