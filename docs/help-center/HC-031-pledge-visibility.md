# Pledge and total visibility settings

**Article ID:** HC-031  
**Audience:** Registry Owners and Registry Participants

Gift registries may mix:

- Line item **gift** availability  
- **Cash funds** blocks with textual instructions  
- **Group pooled pledges on an item** (with **Pledge Initiator** and contributing **Registry Participant** workflows)  

Each can have subtly different visibility **before reveal**. This article explains **patterns**. Always trust your **live screens** and confirmed behavior after you interact with toggles or flows.

---

## Cash fund aggregates (Registry Owner facing)

**Registry Owners** sometimes control whether **overall pledged totals per fund** are visible **before reveal** **without** exposing individual payer identities.

If enabled, **Registry Owners** may infer momentum (“we are halfway”) while still not knowing granular donors until reveal, or depending on interplay with other pledging channels.

If disabled, totals may be withheld until reveal for stricter secrecy.

---

## Item level group pledges

**Registry Participants** looking at eligible items may see **gathered pool sums** aiding coordination while **Registry Owner** identity revelations still follow broader reveal rules (**confirm in your running app**).

Payout instructions and receipt artifacts heighten sensitivities, even when classical “surprise gifting” narration focuses on **who** bought **what** physical item.

Therefore read **[Group pledges and sensitive payout information](HC-024-pledges-group-gifts-sensitive-info.md)**, not only this article.

---

## Why settings differ by feature type

Funds versus item pools evolved different engineering payloads. Do not assume toggles meant for **cash funds** automatically reshape **structured Pledge Initiator and Registry Participant secrecy** arcs identically. The product should label clearly. File UX bugs if ambiguity harms comprehension.

---

## Consumer transparency principle

Misleading dashboards implying funds “safely escrowed internally” **when they are actually only logs** harms fairness. Marketing and UI should mirror **[Terms of use](../legal/terms-of-service.md)** (what Beabr is and is not).
